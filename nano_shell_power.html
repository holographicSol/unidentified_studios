⚛️ Physics Basis

Quantum Tunneling Enhancement

At nano-scales (1-100 nm), electrons in CNTs and nanowires exhibit quantum tunneling across potential barriers that would block classical electron flow. This reduces effective resistance and enables current flow at lower voltages. Estimated gain: 3-5x.

Metamaterial Resonance

Split-ring resonators (SRRs) embedded in the nano-lattice are engineered to resonant frequencies matching the target electromagnetic field. When driven at resonance, impedance drops dramatically and energy coupling increases. With 1,000 different resonances tuned across 10^-3 Hz to 10^12 Hz, the shell captures energy across the entire spectrum, not just one frequency.

Coherent Phase Alignment

Nano-elements can be phase-synchronized through feedback networks embedded in the lattice. All elements oscillate in phase with the incident field, creating constructive interference. Current from 10^15 elements adds linearly instead of randomly (which would give only √N enhancement).

Surface Plasmon Resonance

Metallic nano-elements (Ag, Cu, Al nanowires) support surface plasmon polaritons (SPPs)—collective electron oscillations at metal surfaces. These couple strongly to EM fields and create "hot spots" of high field intensity. Effective field seen by electrons: 10-100x stronger than free space field.

⚠️ Caveat: These calculations assume ideal nano-material properties and perfect coherence. Real materials would have defects, thermal losses, and fabrication imperfections. Realistic efficiency: 50-70% of theoretical maximum. Even at 50%, the nano-shell still outperforms copper wire arrays by 5,000+x.
